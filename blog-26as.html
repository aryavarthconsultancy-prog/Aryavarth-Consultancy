Resolving Form 26AS & AIS Mismatches

The Annual Information Statement (AIS) and Form 26AS are critical documents when filing your Income Tax Return. A mismatch between these two or against your actual income can lead to automated tax notices from the Income Tax Department.

Why Mismatches Occur

Mismatches usually happen due to errors in reporting by deductors (banks, employers, property buyers), delayed updates of TDS/TCS, or duplication of entries in the AIS.

Practical Steps to Resolve

  • Identify the Discrepancy: Download both Form 26AS and AIS from the income tax portal and compare them line-by-line.
  • Submit Feedback on AIS: If you find an entry in the AIS that does not belong to you or is duplicated, use the 'Provide Feedback' option on the portal to contest it.
  • Contact the Deductor: If Form 26AS is missing TDS that was deducted, contact the deductor and request them to file a revised TDS return.
  • Retain Documentation: Always keep proofs like bank statements, sale deeds, and TDS certificates handy in case of a scrutiny notice.
