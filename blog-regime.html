New Tax Regime vs Old Tax Regime

Choosing between the Old and New Tax Regime is one of the most common dilemmas taxpayers face today. With the government pushing for a simplified tax structure, the New Regime offers lower tax rates but at the cost of giving up most deductions.

The Old Tax Regime

The old regime is highly beneficial for individuals who have structured investments. If you claim high deductions under Section 80C (PPF, ELSS, LIC), Section 80D (Health Insurance), HRA, and Home Loan Interest (Section 24), the old regime usually results in a lower net tax liability.

The New Tax Regime

The new regime provides reduced tax slab rates. It is the default regime and is highly recommended for individuals with minimal to no tax-saving investments or those who want a hassle-free filing experience without locking their money into 80C instruments.

How to Choose?

  • Calculate your total deductions (80C, 80D, HRA, LTA).
  • Calculate your gross tax liability under both regimes.
  • If your total deductions exceed a certain "breakeven" point (typically around ₹3.75 Lakhs for higher brackets), the Old Regime is better. Otherwise, switch to the New Regime.
