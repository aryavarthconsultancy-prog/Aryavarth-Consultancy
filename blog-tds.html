Claiming TDS Refund (No Taxable Income)

Tax Deducted at Source (TDS) is often deducted by banks on Fixed Deposit interest or by companies paying dividends, even if your total annual income falls below the basic exemption limit (₹3 Lakhs under the new regime).

Why was TDS Deducted?

Banks automatically deduct a 10% TDS on FD interest if it exceeds ₹40,000 in a year (₹50,000 for senior citizens). If you forgot to submit Form 15G or 15H at the beginning of the financial year, the bank is legally obligated to deduct this tax.

How to Claim it Back

  • Verify 26AS: First, ensure the deducted TDS reflects in your Form 26AS and AIS on the Income Tax Portal.
  • File your ITR: To get the money back, you must file an Income Tax Return (usually ITR-1) for the respective assessment year.
  • Declare Income: Declare the FD interest under "Income from Other Sources". The portal will automatically calculate your tax liability as Zero.
  • Refund Processing: The deducted amount will show up as a "Refund Due". Once you e-verify the return, the IT department will process it and credit the refund directly to your pre-validated bank account.
