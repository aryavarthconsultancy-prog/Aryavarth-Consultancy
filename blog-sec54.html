Maximizing Section 54 Exemption

Selling a residential property often triggers a substantial Long-Term Capital Gains (LTCG) tax liability. However, Section 54 of the Income Tax Act provides a lucrative avenue for individuals to legally save on this tax by reinvesting the gains into a new residential property.

Key Conditions for Exemption

  • Asset Type: The asset sold must be a long-term residential house property (held for more than 24 months).
  • Reinvestment: The capital gains must be invested in purchasing another residential house property within 1 year before or 2 years after the sale date, or constructing one within 3 years.
  • Number of Properties: The exemption is generally available for reinvestment in ONE residential house in India. A once-in-a-lifetime exception allows reinvestment in TWO houses if the gains do not exceed ₹2 crores.

Capital Gains Account Scheme (CGAS)

If you are unable to reinvest the amount before filing your income tax return (or before the due date of filing), you must deposit the unutilized capital gains into a Capital Gains Account Scheme with an authorized bank to claim the exemption.
