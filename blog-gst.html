Handling GST ITC Mismatch Notices

One of the most common notices under GST is regarding the mismatch of Input Tax Credit (ITC) claimed in GSTR-3B versus the credit available in GSTR-2A/2B. Receiving this notice can be stressful, but handling it correctly is crucial to avoid heavy penalties and interest.

Why Does This Mismatch Occur?

  • Your supplier failed to file their GSTR-1 or filed it late.
  • Your supplier made an error while uploading B2B invoices (e.g., incorrect GSTIN, invoice number, or tax amount).
  • You claimed ITC based on a purchase register without reconciling it with GSTR-2B.

Steps to Resolve an ITC Mismatch Notice

1. Thorough Reconciliation: The first step is to download the Excel version of your GSTR-2A/2B for the given period and reconcile it against your purchase register. Identify the specific invoices that are missing or mismatched.

2. Follow Up with Suppliers: For missing invoices, immediately contact your suppliers. Ask them to file their returns or amend the erroneous entries in their next GSTR-1 filing.

3. Drafting the Reply: Prepare a comprehensive reply to the GST officer. Attach the reconciliation statement, copies of valid tax invoices, e-way bills, and proof of payment made to the supplier, establishing that you are a genuine buyer and fulfill all conditions of Section 16 of the CGST Act.
